Resilience of Autonomous Systems

We consider a system of nominal dynamics $$\dot{x}(t) = f\big(t, x(t)\big) + g\big(t, x(t)\big) \bar{B} \bar{u}(t), \qquad x(0) = x_0 \in \mathbb{R}^n,$$ with constant matrix $\bar{B} \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times (m+p)}$ and control signal $\bar{u}(t) \in \bar{\mathcal{U}}$.

After a partial loss of control authority, $p$ of the initial $m+p$ actuators of the system are producing uncontrolled and possibly undesirable outputs within their full actuation range. We split $\bar{B}$ into the controlled actuators $B$ and the malfunctioning ones $C$. Similarly, signal $\bar{u}$ is split into the controls $u(t) \in \mathcal{U}$ and the undesirable inputs $w(t) \in \mathcal{W}$. The malfunctioning dynamics become $$\dot{x}(t) = f\big(t, x(t)\big) + g\big(t, x(t)\big) \big(Bu(t) + Cw(t) \big), \qquad x(0) = x_0 \in \mathbb{R}^n.$$

This malfunction leads to several problems of interest:

  • can the malfunctioning system resiliently reach a given target?
  • over which actuators can the system resiliently lose control?
  • how to design a system resilient to the loss of control over any one of its actuators?
  • how to quantify the resilience of a system?
I answered all of these questions during my PhD.

Resilient Reachability of Linear Driftless Systems
 We started with linear driftless nominal dynamics $$\dot{x}(t) = \bar{B} \bar{u}(t), \qquad x(0) = x_0 \in \mathbb{R}^n, \qquad \bar{u}(t) \in \bar{\mathcal{U}}.$$ After a partial loss of control authority over actuators, the malfunctioning dynamics become $$\dot{x}(t) = Bu(t) + Cw(t), \qquad x(0) = x_0 \in \mathbb{R}^n,$$ with control $u(t) \in \mathcal{U}$ and undesirable input $w(t) \in \mathcal{W}$.
  • A target $x_g$ is resiliently reachable if for all undesirable input $w$ there exists a control input $u$ driving the state of the malfunctioning system from $x_0$ to $x_g$.
  • We derived analytical conditions for the nominal system to be resilient, i.e., to be able to resiliently reach any target.
  • This work has been presented at the 2020 IFAC World Congress.
Designing Resilient Linear Systems
 With the same linear driftless dynamics as above, we showed that a system in dimension $n$ needs at least $2n+1$ actuators to be resilient to the loss of control over any one of them. We also showed that the minimal size for a driftless system to be 2-resilient is nonlinear in $n$. This work has been published in the IEEE Transactions of Automatic Control.
Quantitative Resilience of Linear Driftless Systems
 To quantify how much longer the malfunctioning system needs to reach a target $x_g \in \mathbb{R}^n$ compared to the nominal dynamics, we introduce the nominal reach time $$T_N^*(x_g) := \underset{\bar{u}(t) \, \in \, \bar{\mathcal{U}} }{\inf} \left\{ T : x_g - x_0 = \int_0^T \bar{B} \bar{u}(t) dt \right\},$$ the malfunctioning reach time $$T_M^*(x_g) := \underset{w(t) \, \in \, \mathcal{W} }{\sup} \left\{ \underset{u(t) \, \in \, \mathcal{U} }{\inf} \left\{ T : x_g - x_0 = \int_0^T Bu(t) + Cw(t) dt \right\} \right\},$$ and the quantitative resilience of the system $$r_q := \underset{x_g \, \in \, \mathbb{R}^n}{\inf} \frac{T_N^*(x_g)}{T_M^*(x_g)}.$$
  • Relying on time-optimal control theory we simplified the expressions of the reach times $T_N^*$ and $T_M^*$.
  • We established the Maximax Minimax Quotient Theorem to solve the nonlinear optimization over $x_g$ in $r_q$.
  • This work has first been presented at the 2021 SIAM Conference on Control and its Applications and a more complete version has been published in the IEEE Transactions of Automatic Control.
The Maximax Minimax Quotient Theorem
 The problem of quantifying the resilience of driftless linear systems leads to a nonlinear optimization problem consisting of four nested optimizations : $$\underset{d\, \in\, \mathbb{S}}{\max}\ \frac{\underset{x\, \in\, \mathcal{X},\ y\, \in\, \mathcal{Y}}{\max} \big\{ \|x + y\| : x + y \in \mathbb{R}^+d \big\} }{ \underset{x\, \in\, \mathcal{X}}{\min} \big\{ \underset{y\, \in\, \mathcal{Y}}{\max} \big\{ \|x + y\| : x + y \in \mathbb{R}^+d \big\} \big\} },$$ where $\mathcal{Y} := \big\{ Bu : u \in \mathcal{U} \big\}$ represents the admissible control inputs, while $\mathcal{X} := \big\{ Cw : w \in \mathcal{W} \big\}$ represents the undesirable and uncontrolled inputs.
  • We developed a geometrical solution to this optimization in the case $\dim(\mathcal{X}) = 1$ as illustrated below.
  • This work has been published in the Journal of Optimization Theory and Applications.

Resilience of Linear Systems
 We extended our work to non-driftless dynamics $$\dot{x}(t) = Ax(t) + \bar{B} \bar{u}(t), \qquad x(0) = x_0 \in \mathbb{R}^n, \qquad \bar{u}(t) \in \bar{\mathcal{U}}.$$ After a partial loss of control authority over actuators, the malfunctioning dynamics become $$\dot{x}(t) = Ax(t) + Bu(t) + Cw(t), \qquad x(0) = x_0 \in \mathbb{R}^n,$$ with control $u(t) \in \mathcal{U}$ and undesirable input $w(t) \in \mathcal{W}$. To quantify how much longer the malfunctioning system needs to reach a target $x_g \in \mathbb{R}^n$ compared to the nominal dynamics, we introduce the nominal reach time $$T_N^*(x_0, x_g) := \underset{\bar{u}(t) \, \in \, \bar{\mathcal{U}} }{\inf} \left\{ T \geq 0 : x_g = e^{AT}\left( x_0 + \int_0^T e^{-At}\bar{B} \bar{u}(t) dt\right) \right\},$$ the malfunctioning reach time $$T_M^*(x_0, x_g) := \underset{w(t) \, \in \, \mathcal{W} }{\sup} \left\{ \underset{u(t) \, \in \, \mathcal{U} }{\inf} \left\{ T \geq 0 : x_g = e^{AT}\left( x_0 + \int_0^T e^{-At}\big(Bu(t) + Cw(t) \big)dt \right)\right\} \right\},$$ and the quantitative resilience of the system $$r_q := \underset{x_0 \, \in \, \mathbb{R}^n, \ x_g \, \in \, \mathbb{R}^n}{\inf} \frac{T_N^*(x_0, x_g)}{T_M^*(x_0, x_g)}.$$
  • Relying on Lyapunov theory we derived analytical bounds on $T_N^*$, $T_M^*$ and $r_q$.
  • This work has first been presented at the 2022 European Control Conference and a more complete version has been published in Automatica.
Resilience of an Orbital Inspection Mission
  • We demonstrate the resilience of a spacecraft to perform an orbital inspection mission depsite the loss of control authority over one of its thrusters and with a controller further plagued by actuation delay.
  • This work has been presented at the 2022 AAS/AIAA Astrodynamics Specialists Conference.
